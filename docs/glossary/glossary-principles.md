# Glossary Principles

Glossaries are the highest-leverage improvement for long-form MTL reading.

## Core Rule

Consistency usually matters more than finding the single perfect English term.

## What To Lock Early

- protagonist and major character names;
- sects, clans, dynasties, kingdoms, factions;
- cultivation realms and rank ladders;
- signature techniques, items, and titles;
- recurring place names.

## Naming Decisions

Use one of these strategies and stay consistent:

- transliteration: `Qinglan Sect`;
- translated meaning: `Azure Mist Sect`;
- mixed style: transliterated proper noun plus translated suffix.

## Recommended Default

For many xianxia and progression-fantasy novels, a mixed style is easier to maintain:

- transliterate person names;
- often transliterate core cultural terms like `qi`, `dao`, `dantian`;
- translate structural labels like `Sect`, `Palace`, `Kingdom`, `Manual`, `Hall`.

## Bad Signs

Your glossary is too weak if the same term becomes multiple English variants in the first 20 chapters.

## Suggested Table Format

| Chinese | Preferred English | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 炼气 | Qi Refining | keep stable across all chapters |
| 筑基 | Foundation Establishment | avoid Base Building |
| 青岚宗 | Qinglan Sect | sect suffix translated |
