# Wuxia Reading Guide

Wuxia is different from xianxia. It is usually more grounded in martial arts traditions, sect rivalries, jianghu culture, title systems, and period-style social language.

## What Makes Wuxia Hard For MTL

- honorifics and martial titles are inconsistent;
- sect and school names can be translated too literally;
- jianghu concepts lose tone when flattened into generic fantasy English;
- martial arts move names are often awkward in raw MTL.

## What To Lock Early

- school or sect names;
- martial ranks and honorifics;
- recurring weapon names;
- move and technique naming style;
- jianghu-specific terms.

## Recommended Translation Bias

Wuxia often benefits from cleaner English than xianxia, but it still needs cultural texture.

A good default:

- keep core social and martial terms when they carry cultural meaning;
- translate structure words clearly;
- avoid turning everything into generic medieval fantasy phrasing.

## Practical Rule

If the story is driven by martial reputation, lineage, schools, and social rank, title consistency matters almost as much as character names.
