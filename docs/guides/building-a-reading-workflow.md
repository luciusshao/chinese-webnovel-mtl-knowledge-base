# Building A Reading Workflow

The biggest MTL improvement usually does not come from one better prompt. It comes from having a repeatable workflow.

## A Practical Workflow

1. choose one novel;
2. extract recurring names and terms from the first few chapters;
3. build a starter glossary;
4. use one prompt template consistently;
5. review drift every 5 to 10 chapters;
6. update the glossary when the story introduces important new terms.

## What To Save Between Chapters

- character names;
- sects and factions;
- cultivation realms;
- locations;
- important treasures, techniques, and titles.

## When To Intervene Manually

Intervene when:

- names start changing;
- realm names stop matching earlier chapters;
- one key term gets over-translated;
- the output becomes summary-like instead of full translation.

## What Not To Optimize Too Early

Do not spend too much time polishing rare terms before you lock the high-frequency terms.

For long novels, stable basics matter more than perfect edge cases.
