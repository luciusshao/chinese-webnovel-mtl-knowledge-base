# Xianxia Reading Guide

Xianxia is one of the hardest genres for raw MTL because it depends heavily on repeated terminology, hierarchical power systems, sect structures, Dao concepts, and inherited genre vocabulary.

## What Makes Xianxia Hard For MTL

- cultivation realms drift across chapters;
- Dao and qi terminology gets over-translated;
- sect and inheritance systems use many recurring proper nouns;
- treasure, alchemy, and technique names often look similar in English.

## What To Lock Early

Before reading too far, stabilize:

- realm ladder;
- sect names;
- major character names;
- treasure categories;
- alchemy and pill terminology;
- Dao and qi terms.

## Recommended Translation Bias

For xianxia, a partially transliterated style is usually more stable than fully meaning-based translation.

Good examples:

- qi;
- Dao;
- dantian;
- Nascent Soul;
- Foundation Establishment.

## Common Failure Mode

The same cultivation stage gets translated three different ways. Once that happens, readers start mentally re-editing every chapter.

## Practical Rule

In xianxia, consistency usually matters more than elegance.
