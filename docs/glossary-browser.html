Search Chinese-English web novel terms

The browser above lets you search across Chinese characters, pinyin in notes, and preferred English translations at once — useful when an MTL output gives you a romanisation like jindan and you want to check the recommended English form (Golden Core) plus the original term (金丹).

Filter by genre: xianxia, wuxia, xiuxian

Each entry is tagged with one of three genres so you can narrow the table to the conventions of the novel you're reading. Prefer a static page? Read the standalone lists: Xianxia, Wuxia, Xiuxian.

Export glossary terms as CSV or Markdown

Tick the rows you need, then click Export selected as CSV for a spreadsheet-friendly file, or Export as Markdown for a pasteable table you can drop into ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or any LLM prompt. For a ready-made starter pack instead, head to the Glossary Library.

How to use this glossary for MTL reading

A consistent glossary is the single biggest lever for readable machine translation of Chinese web novels. Pick the genre that matches your novel, export the terms you'll see most often, and feed that list to your translator as a glossary or system prompt. New term not on the list? Contribute it and the next reader will benefit too.
